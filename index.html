Two-Tier Alert Logic

TIER 1 — "Heads Up" Sensitive, delta-based — fires early
Fire when ANY 2 of these day-over-day deltas are true on the same day:
• RHR Δ ≥ +4 bpm in one day
• Walking HR Δ ≥ +5 bpm in one day (both days ≤ 90 bpm, filtering exercise)
• HRV Δ ≤ -15% in one day
• Wrist Temp Δ ≥ +0.4°F in one day
→ Jan 21: RHR Δ = +4 ✓, Walking HR Δ = +7 ✓ → FIRES

Why Delta-Based Detection Works Earlier

💡
Absolute thresholds wait for big deviations. RHR has to climb +6 bpm above your rolling average before an absolute rule notices. That takes 1-2 days of ramping. But a +4 bpm single-day jump is abnormal regardless of where you started — it reflects a sudden autonomic shift, which is exactly what happens when your immune system activates.
💡
Walking HR amplifies subtle signals. At rest, your autonomic system can mostly compensate for early immune activation. But during walking (a mild stressor), the compensation fails — your heart has to work harder. That's why Walking HR jumped +7 bpm on Jan 21 while resting metrics were still borderline. It's a "stress test" your watch does for free every day.
💡
The 2-of-4 rule controls false positives. A single metric spiking (e.g., Walking HR after a hard workout) shouldn't trigger an alert. But two independent metrics jumping simultaneously — cardiac at rest (RHR) AND cardiac under load (Walking HR) — is unlikely to be noise. In your data, the only day this co-fired in the entire window was Jan 21.
