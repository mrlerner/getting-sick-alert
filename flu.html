Retrospective analysis of Whoop data before Influenza A diagnosis — February 23, 2026

Key Finding: HRV-CV (Coefficient of Variation) was the best early signal

Traditional metrics (RHR, HRV absolute) showed no clear warning before symptoms. But HRV-CV — the consistency of HRV over a 7-day window — compressed from ~22% to 11.4% in the week before illness. The autonomic nervous system lost its dynamic range days before the flu hit.

⚠️

Possible "Heads Up" — Wednesday Feb 18

HRV-CV dropped below 15% for 3+ days. Combined with highest baseline RHR (60 bpm) and lowest SpO2 (91.4% on Feb 19). Alert could have fired ~3 days before symptoms.

🚨

Confirmed illness — Monday Feb 23 morning

Recovery crashed to 3%. HRV dropped 44% overnight. RHR spiked to 69. Skin temp hit 36.4°C (fever). Respiratory rate jumped 21%. But by this point symptoms had been present for 30+ hours.

Findings — Ranked by Early Warning Value

1
7-Day HRV-CV compressed from ~22% to 11.4% (Feb 15→21): This was the only metric that showed a sustained pre-illness pattern. HRV stopped oscillating naturally and got trapped in a narrow band (36-51 ms). The CV dropped below 15% starting Feb 15 and hit its nadir of 11.4% on Feb 21 (day of first symptoms). A threshold of "CV < 15% for 3+ consecutive days" would have fired around Feb 18 — giving ~3 days of lead time.
2
RHR crept up to 60 bpm on Feb 18 (highest baseline): Baseline RHR was ~56 bpm. The Feb 18 reading of 60 was the highest non-sick value, coinciding with the lowest non-sick HRV (36.6 ms). Together with the CV compression, this was the most suspicious pre-illness day. However, RHR alone showed only a +4 bpm shift — within normal variation.
3
SpO2 dipped to 91.4% on Feb 19: The lowest reading in the entire dataset. Combined with the HRV-CV compression and elevated RHR the day before, this was the third signal in the Feb 18-19 cluster. However, SpO2 readings from wrist-based sensors are noisy, so this alone wouldn't justify an alert.
4
Avg daytime HR rose from 61 → 66 → 69 (Feb 20→22): The same daily activities cost more cardiac effort as the immune system activated. The Feb 21 reading of 66 bpm was the first day of symptoms — but there was also a flight that day, which confounds the signal.
5
Skin temp, respiratory rate, and recovery all collapsed on Feb 23 — lagging indicators: Skin temp spiked to 36.4°C (fever), respiratory rate jumped 21% to 16.8 breaths/min, and recovery crashed to 3%. All powerful confirmation that illness had arrived, but none provided advance warning. symptoms had been present for 30+ hours before these metrics moved.

Proposed Alert Logic

⚡
Primary trigger: 7-day HRV-CV < 15% for ≥ 3 consecutive days AND RHR > 7-day rolling average. This would have fired on Feb 18 — giving ~3 days of lead time before first symptoms.
🔄
Confirmation signals: SpO2 < 93%, daytime avg HR > baseline + 3 bpm, or skin temp > baseline + 0.5°C. Any 1 confirming = higher confidence.
💊
Recommended actions on alert: Start antiviral (oseltamivir/Tamiflu within 48hrs of exposure is most effective), zinc, reduce exertion, extra sleep, hydrate.
